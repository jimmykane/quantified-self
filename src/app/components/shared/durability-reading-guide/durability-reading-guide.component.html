How to read durability

It compares the usable first and second parts of a long, reasonably steady aerobic effort. For running, cycling, and open-water swimming, warm-up and cool-down are excluded and both output and heart rate must be recorded.

For one activity

Less power or speed for the same heart rate later in the session can suggest a fade if you meant to hold a similar effort. If you deliberately eased off, changed terrain, coasted, or changed pace, it simply describes that pacing choice.

For swimming

Pool swims compare like-for-like pace and, when available, SWOLF from the same stroke and pool length. A missing result means there was no suitable comparison, not zero durability.

This is context for your training, not a fitness verdict or workout prescription.
